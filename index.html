Roberto Clemente

First Latino inducted into Baseball Hall of Fame

Here's a timeline of Roberto Clemente's life:

  • 1934 - Born August 18th in Carolina, Puerto Rico.
  • 1952 - At age 18, Clemente signs his first baseball contract with Los Cangrejeros de Santurce in the Puerto Rican league for $5,000.
  • 1953 - The Brooklyn Dodgers, which helped break the color barrier with the signing of Jackie Robinson, sign Clemente for $10,000.
  • 1954 - The Pittsburgh Pirates choose Clemente in first round of the minor league draft after a year playing with the Dodger's minor league affiliate in Montreal.
  • 1955 - On April 17th, Clemente makes his major league debut with the Pittsburgh Pirates.
  • 1960 - The Pittsburgh Pirates win the World Series. Clemente feels slighted after finishing 8th in the National League Most Valuable Player voting.
  • 1961 - Clemente records 27 outfield assists and wins the first of 12 consecutive Gold Gloves for his defensive play in right field. Clemente also leads the National League in batting, finishing the season with a .351 average.
  • 1964 - Clemente wins a second NL batting title with a .339 average.
  • 1964 - On November 14th, Clemente marries Vera Cristina Zabala in San Juan, Puerto Rico. They will go on to have three children: Roberto Jr., Luis Roberto and Enrique Roberto.
  • 1967 - At baseball's Winter Meetings, Clemente is declared "the best player in baseball today" by a plurality of GMs.
  • 1971 - Clemente helped win the World Series for the Pittsburgh Pirates for the second time. On this occasion, he received the World Series Most Valuable Player Award.
  • 1972 - On October 3rd, Clemente plays his last major league game with the Pittsburgh Pirates.
  • 1972 - On December 31st, Clemente dies at age 38 in a plane crash while en route to deliver aid to earthquake victims in Nicaragua.
  • 1973 - Clemente is inducted into the Baseball Hall of Fame. This made him the first player from Latin America and the Caribbean to be bestowed with this honor.

"Every sports fan admired and respected Roberto Clemente as one of the greatest baseball players of our time. In the tragedy of his untimely death, we are reminded that he deserved even greater respect and admiration for his splendid qualities as a generous and kind human being. He sacrificed his life on a mission of mercy. The best memorial we can build to his memory is to contribute generously for the relief of those he was trying to help - the earthquake victims in Nicaragua."

-- US President Richard Nixon
